Scenario

A town in Tamil Nadu on the banks of the Amaravathi River is experiencing acute water management challenges. Over-extraction of water from the river for agriculture, industry, and domestic use has led to a significant reduction in river flow, affecting both water quality and availability. Seasonal variations in rainfall further compound the problem, leading to water shortages during dry months.

The town relies on a combination of the Amaravathi River and borewells, but groundwater levels are depleting rapidly, dropping by 2-3 meters annually. The existing water supply infrastructure is outdated, with substantial transmission losses and inadequate coverage, leaving 40% of households dependent on private tankers or borewells. To address these issues, a comprehensive water supply project is being planned. The project includes:

  • Constructing new water treatment plants to meet future demand.
  • Upgrading the distribution network to improve efficiency and reduce losses.
  • Introducing SCADA technology for real-time monitoring and control.
  • Developing sustainable practices to rejuvenate the Amaravathi River and protect its ecosystem.

As part of the planning team, your role is to identify the challenges and risks, evaluate mitigation measures, and ensure compliance with Environmental, Climate Change, and Social Management Framework (ESCMF) guidelines.
